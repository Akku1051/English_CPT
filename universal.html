Universal Connections



The universal implications of these three literary works clearly highlight that many people experience moments of deep internal turmoil and conflict, which are important parts of being a human. However, it is only through confronting these struggles, much like Saul's journey of acceptance in Indian Horse or Jing Mei's self-discovery in "Two Kinds," that individuals can begin to heal, grow, and find resolution. In contrast, when one avoids facing these inner battles, the consequences can be dire, as showed by Macbeth's madness. His ambition and refusal to address his guilt and moral conflict ultimately lead to his self-destruction, serving as a example of the dangers of evasion and denial.
